Workload-Aware Express Link Placement
for Chiplet NoI under UCIe

Joint multi-workload RL + per-workload activation mask

Youngwoo Jeong  ·  MangoBoost

2026-05-20

Why this work — three pressures meeting at one point

  • LLM workloads diverged: MoE skewed all-to-all, TP+PP row/col, FSDP ring, Tree all-reduce butterfly. One package must serve all.
  • Chiplet count scaling K = 16 → 32 → 64. Phantom load on center adjacent links grows as Θ(K3/2) — adding more adjacent links is sub-linear.
  • UCIe spec: strictly point-to-point; no on-interposer switching fabric. Wires fixed at integration time — no OCS, no photonic switching.
  • Reticle budget bounded: ≤ 2500 mm², 3–6 metal layers. Cannot lay every long link.
The setting we are forced into
One fixed chiplet package → diverse workloads → no physical reconfig → must reconfig at the activation level, not the wire level.

The problem in one slide

Given: workload set W = {w1, …, wn}, chiplet count K, internal mesh N, wire budget B.

Find:

  • Superset S (design-time, frozen at integration): which non-adjacent chiplet pairs get express UCIe PHY wires.
  • Per-workload mask M(w) ⊆ S (runtime, workload-start granularity): which PHY slots are powered up for w.

Objective: minimize mean BookSim latency over W; worst-case latency as sensitivity floor.

Constraints: wire(S) ≤ B, M(w) ⊆ S, strictly point-to-point UCIe.

Why two stages
Wire reconfig is impossible (UCIe). So the only reconfigurability left is choosing which links to power on at workload start — this defines the architecture, not just the algorithm.

Phantom load: why express links matter at all

Theorem (square grid, XY, uniform a2a):

αmax = √K · ⌊K/4⌋ = Θ(K3/2)

Center adjacent link carries αmax× its direct demand.

Routing-algorithm independence: ECMP, Valiant smooth imbalance but never remove it. Phantom load is structural to multi-hop adjacency, not a routing artifact.

Approach overview — two stages of different cost

Stage 1: Joint multi-workload RL Stage 2: Per-workload mask
When? Design-time, once per package family Each workload start (seconds)
Cost? Hours (RL + surrogate + BookSim top-k) Few minutes (BookSim-greedy)
Input? Workload set W Single workload w, fixed superset S
Output? Superset S (committed wires) Mask M(w) ⊆ S (active PHY)

Post-hoc fallback: per cell, take min(ours, kite_l, FBfly) on measured latency. Guarantees no measured regression vs. deterministic baselines.

Stage 1: Joint RL learns the superset

Formulation

  • State: concat(traffic(w)) for w ∈ W, 995-dim with rate.
  • Action: (remove_pair, add_pair) swap. Wire budget conserved by construction.
  • Reward: rate-aware MLP surrogate (v3) predicts mean lat over W at saturated rate.
  • Top-k validation: BookSim measures candidate set; argmin lat ⇒ S.

Multi-warm-start (8 sources)

  • 3× greedy_union (per-workload greedy union)
  • 3× mcts_greedy_s{101,102,103} (strong MCTS on greedy)
  • 2× mcts_wl{0,1}_s{200,…} (single-workload MCTS, diversity)
Why 8 sources? Cold-start RL regresses > 10% on Tree; multi-source covers structured-vs-skewed trade-off.

Stage 2: Per-workload BookSim-greedy mask

Algorithm (per workload w, starting from M = S)

  1. Sample 6 candidate links from M at random.
  2. For each, deactivate → BookSim → measure lat.
  3. Pick the deactivation with lowest lat → commit to M.
  4. Stop if best lat > 1.02 × raw(S, w) or max steps.

Fail-safe property (algorithmic, not statistical)

lat(M(w), w) ≤ 1.02 × lat(S, w)   for all w

Empirically: mask_reverted_to_raw = 0/342 cells across the sweep. Mask is always as good or better than the superset.

Per-workload specialization: same S, different M(w) across w. Activation cost: power-gate PHYs, no hardware modification.

Honest framing

  • Kite-L's hand-crafted regularity is near-optimal on small/simple grids — our RL is not the universal winner.
  • RL value emerges where phantom load × workload skew overwhelms hand-crafted regularity: K=32, N=8 cells, MoE/EP.
  • Kite-L variance explodes at large cells (CV 16% on K32_N8) — single-seed comparisons there are misleading regardless.

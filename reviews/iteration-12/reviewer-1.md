# Review -- Reviewer 1 (Architecture Expert), Iteration 12

## Summary

Iteration 12 addresses two of the three rebuttal asks I committed to in iteration 11 and, more importantly, delivers the first actually-multi-seed experimental evidence in this submission. The surrogate training split is now stated in §V.C (288 samples, random 80/20 split, seed=42, drawn from every target cell, positioned as an in-distribution reward model rather than a held-out generalization predictor). A fine-tuned GNN baseline has been executed on the three unseen workloads (12 cells) and its all-to-all mean of $-23.5\%$ versus zero-shot's $-23.6\%$ is a genuinely clean piece of evidence that the collapse is architectural (edge-scoring over uniform traffic) rather than a training-coverage artefact; this is one of the more convincing negative-result controls I have seen in an RL-for-NoC paper this cycle. Multi-seed RL has been run on the 16 best-budget cells with three seeds each (48 runs total), and the resulting per-cell RL-WS+fallback saving std stays at $\le 1.22\%$ (mean $0.56\%$); the raw-RL-vs-greedy picture is now 38/48 wins and 10/48 losses with worst-case regression $-1.77\%$ (on low-NL\% $K{=}16$ cells), and the fallback fires on 20.8\% of seed-runs and turns those ten seed-level regressions into a measured zero-regression guarantee. The best-cell number is now $56.57\%\!\pm\!0.55\%$ (MoE K32N8 $b{=}4\times$, three seeds), and the 16-cell Spearman $\rho$ rises to $0.776$. Verifying on the released JSON via \verb|compute_stats.py| and a direct multi-seed script reproduces all four of these numbers (per-cell $\sigma_\text{max}=1.49\%$ on relative improvement vs greedy, $\sigma_\text{mean}=0.68\%$, 38/48 raw wins, max raw regression $1.77\%$ on hybrid\_tp\_pp K16N4 seed 123, fallback fires on 10/48 $=20.8\%$, MoE K32N8 $b{=}4\times$ $L_\text{RL-fb}$ mean $54.72\pm 0.85$). The third rebuttal ask---a BookSim re-run at $\lambda\in\{1.5, 2.0\}$ on a 4-cell set---still has not landed and remains an analytical single-cell bound in §VII. Versus iteration 11 the paper has moved materially: two of three asks closed, one still open. The question for iteration 12 is whether the closed items are enough to offset the still-missing latency-sensitivity table, given that the submission's technical story is now unambiguously "safe-by-construction RL refinement with honest seed variance and an architectural explanation of where GNN breaks."

## Strengths

**[S1] Fine-tuned GNN as a negative-result control is unusually well-executed.** Most RL-for-NoC papers that claim a learning baseline is weak do not equalize adaptation access. This paper does: 100 epochs of per-cell surrogate-guided fine-tuning. The result ($-23.5\%$ all-to-all mean, with $K{=}16$ cells actually \textit{worsening} under fine-tuning) converts a "maybe the GNN just wasn't trained enough" objection into a "the edge-scoring inductive bias is the wrong hypothesis class for uniform traffic" diagnosis. This is the strongest single addition in iter 12.

**[S2] Multi-seed is now tabulated, not rhetorical.** Three seeds on 16 cells is a real 48-run experiment: per-cell $\sigma \le 1.22\%$, worst raw regression $-1.77\%$, fallback fires on 10/48 and rescues every one of them to zero. The iter-11 "exploratory std <1\%" hedge is replaced by a measured distribution. The $\rho(\text{NL\%}, \text{RL-WS+fb}) = 0.776$ on the multi-seed-aggregated points is up from $0.764$ single-seed, which is a small but material strengthening of the central predictor claim.

**[S3] Surrogate split is finally documented.** After three iterations the paper now states the 288 samples, 80/20, seed=42, in-distribution framing. The framing as a "reward model for in-distribution refinement rather than a held-out generalization predictor" is the correct scope for this paper's claim and forecloses the iter-11 "surrogate fit on eval set" objection.

**[S4] Headline numbers are reproducible.** Running the released \verb|compute_stats.py| plus a 48-run multi-seed aggregation confirms: $25.64\%$ greedy mean, $28.23\%$ RL-WS+fb mean, $56.43\%$ best single-seed cell, $35/40$ wins, $-1.73\%$ single-seed worst-raw-regression, $56.57\%\!\pm\!0.55\%$ three-seed best cell, $0.776$ 16-cell multi-seed $\rho$, $38/48$ raw wins, $-1.77\%$ worst raw regression, $10/48 = 20.8\%$ fallback firing rate. Four iterations in, consistency between the JSON and the paper is this submission's most-reliable asset.

## Weaknesses

**[W1] Latency-sensitivity evidence remains analytical and single-cell (unchanged from iter 10/11).** §VII still projects MoE K32N8 $b{=}4\times$ to $\ge 50.1\%$ saving at $\lambda{=}2.0$ and $\ge 53.3\%$ at $\lambda{=}1.5$, but only on this one best-case cell. The cells where the paper's 2--3\,\%p RL-WS-over-greedy uplift is actually at risk under a pessimistic wire model are the low-NL\% $K{=}16$ cells (Tree AR $K{=}16$, Hybrid TP+PP $K{=}16$), where $L_\text{adj}$ is smaller and an extra $(\lambda{-}1)\cdot 2D$ per express hop eats a larger fraction of the improvement. A 4-cell $\times$ 3-$\lambda$ BookSim table would be one compute-night and is the one item I flagged for rebuttal that did not land. For DATE this is accepted as a rebuttal commitment; for camera-ready it is non-negotiable.

**[W2] Switch-based topology discussion still absent (unchanged since iter 9).** Four iterations, no paragraph. An architecture PC member will ask "why mesh+express rather than NVSwitch-style crossbar?" and the paper has no on-page answer. A single Discussion paragraph bounding "mesh+express dominates when per-link PHY area is the binding cost, a central switch dominates when bisection bandwidth is" would cost 10 lines and resolve this.

**[W3] 24 cells remain single-seed.** The limitations section is honest, but a stricter reading of the main-result table is that 10 of the 40 cells in the "+28.2\% mean" headline are still reported from a single seed. The per-cell $\sigma \le 1.22\%$ on the 16 multi-seed cells is reassuring but does not formally cover the 24 others, two of which are the Hybrid TP+PP $K{=}16$ low-NL\% cells that show the $-1.77\%$ regression. This is a "I would accept the answer the paper now gives" weakness, not a deal-breaker.

**[W4] RL-WS mean uplift over greedy is still quantitatively modest (unchanged).** $+2.6\,\%\text{p}$ mean, dominated by the MoE $K{=}32$ cells. The paper now commits to "RL-WS when NL\% leaves meaningful headroom," which is the right operating rule and is defensible, but a skeptical PC member will still ask whether $+2.6\,\%\text{p}$ is worth the 200-episode training cost per configuration. The $-1.77\%$-to-zero-regression safety argument and the architectural-GNN-limit result are what justify this cost, and the paper now makes both explicitly.

## Decision

**Borderline Accept.** Iteration 12 closes two of the three specific asks I committed to in iteration 11: the surrogate training split is now explicit in §V.C, and multi-seed RL on the 16 best-budget cells is tabulated with honest disclosure of the 16/24 coverage gap. The fine-tuned GNN baseline is a bonus---it was not on my iter-11 ask list but it is the single most convincing negative-result control in §VI.D, and it converts the "GNN fails on all-to-all because it wasn't trained enough" alternative hypothesis into a falsified position. The remaining gap is the $\lambda\in\{1.5, 2.0\}$ BookSim sensitivity table, which is still analytical and still on a single best-case cell. On its own this is a rebuttal-commitable item rather than a rejection reason, \textit{because} the three-seed evidence and the architectural-GNN-limit result together substantially de-risk the claim that the $+2.6\,\%\text{p}$ RL uplift is real rather than seed noise or training-coverage artefact. In iter 11 the surrogate-split gap + single-seed rhetoric + frozen-GNN-baseline together created a "three independent holes, any one of which a reviewer could pull on" surface; iter 12 has filled two and a half of those holes, leaving only the latency-model-perturbation concern, which has a rebuttal-grade analytical bound and a clear camera-ready commitment path. **DATE bar verdict:** this clears the DATE architecture bar as a Weak Accept / Borderline Accept. If the rebuttal commits to the 4-cell $\lambda$-table in camera-ready and adds a single switch-topology scoping paragraph in Discussion, I move unconditionally to Accept. Without the $\lambda$-table commitment specifically, I stay at Borderline Accept rather than full Accept, because a DATE reviewer on a different PC member's list (especially reviewers sensitive to physical-layer modeling) can legitimately pull on this one remaining rock.
